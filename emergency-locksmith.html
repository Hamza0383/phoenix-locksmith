Frequently Asked Questions (Emergency)

Is this really 24/7 or just an answering service?

Really 24/7. When you call at 2am, a live dispatcher answers (not a recording). We have techs on-call around the clock. They're not at the shop â€” they're in service vans ready to respond.

How do I know you're legitimate and not a scam?

Legitimate signs:

  • We give you ETA on the phone (scammers say "20 minutes" no matter where you are)
  • We quote pricing over the phone (scammers say "we'll see when we get there")
  • Our tech arrives in marked company vehicle
  • Tech shows ID badge
  • We verify your ownership before service (scammers will open anyone's door)

Red flags for scams:

  • Refuses to give company name
  • Won't quote price range
  • Shows up in unmarked vehicle
  • Demands cash only
  • Tries to charge 10x normal rates ("emergency surcharge")
What if I can't prove I own the car or live at the house?

For cars: We need your driver's license matching the name on the registration. If registration is in glove box and visible through window, we can see it matches your ID.

For homes: We need ID plus something showing you live there (utility bill, lease, mail addressed to you at that address).

Can't prove it? We can't provide service. This protects you from someone claiming they live in your house and getting us to open it for them.

What if my lockout happens in a bad area and I don't feel safe?

Tell the dispatcher your safety concerns. Options:

  • We can have tech call you when 5 min away so you're not standing outside long
  • If you're in immediate danger, call 911 first, then us
  • You can wait in a nearby business or lit area â€” give dispatcher that address
  • Tech can meet you at alternate location then follow you to your car/home

Your safety comes first.

Can you break my window if needed to save a pet/child?

If child or pet is in immediate danger (extreme heat, medical distress), call 911 first. Emergency services can break window immediately.

We carry tools to unlock cars, which is faster and less damaging than breaking glass. But in true life-threatening situations, 911 should be your first call.

We can help AFTER emergency services secure the situation.

Do I have to pay cash for emergency service?

No. We accept credit cards, debit cards, and mobile payments. Cash is fine too, but not required.

Payment is due after service is complete and you verify everything works. We process cards on-site and give you a receipt.

What if I locked my keys in a running car?

Call immediately and tell dispatcher the car is running. This is a high-priority emergency because:

  • Someone could steal the running vehicle
  • Car could be in gear and roll
  • Exhaust in enclosed garage is dangerous

We'll dispatch the nearest tech immediately.

While waiting:

  • Stay with the vehicle if safe to do so
  • Don't try to break window yourself
  • Keep keys visible so tech knows you're the owner
What happens if you damage my lock or car getting me in?

We use professional non-destructive entry tools designed to avoid damage. Damage is extremely rare.

If damage does occur (which happens in less than 1% of cases), we take full responsibility and cover repair costs.

Our techs are trained to open locks safely. They won't force anything that might break.

When to Call 911 Instead of a Locksmith

Call 911 first in these situations:

  • Child or pet showing distress in locked vehicle (extreme heat, medical issue)
  • Someone is injured and trapped inside/outside
  • Active break-in or intruder situation
  • Fire or gas leak with locked door blocking exit
  • Any immediate life-threatening emergency

Then call us after emergency services secure the situation. We can help with access once the immediate danger is handled.

For standard lockouts (inconvenient but not life-threatening), call us directly.
